🧠 What Is Actually Happening — Bagging vs Boosting

🌲 Random Forest Bagging
  • Many trees trained independently in parallel
  • Each tree sees a random sample (rows + features)
  • Final output = majority voting / averaging
  • Focus: overall correctness (high accuracy)
  • Weakness: can miss rare/minority classes → lower recall
  • Best for: noisy data, interpretability, stable baseline
⚡ XGBoost / 🌿 LightGBM Boosting
  • Models trained sequentially — each corrects previous mistakes
  • Later trees focus on hard-to-classify samples
  • Key difference: XGBoost = level-wise growth, LightGBM = leaf-wise growth
  • Leaf-wise: grows the leaf with highest loss reduction → deeper focus on difficult cases
  • Excels at capturing complex patterns + minority classes
🌿 Why LightGBM often wins
  • Leaf‑wise growth → builds deeper, more expressive trees
  • Naturally focuses on misclassified / rare events
  • Higher recall + F1 without sacrificing too much precision
  • Faster training & lower memory for large datasets
  • In your report: LightGBM achieved highest F1 because it captured hard samples better
📌 Leaf‑wise vs Level‑wise
Level-wise (XGBoost): balanced, grows all leaves equally → safer but less aggressive.
Leaf-wise (LightGBM): grows most promising leaf first → more power on complex patterns.
💡 Bottom line: Random Forest = parallel voting for stability. Boosting = sequential error correction. LightGBM’s leaf-wise strategy gives it an edge on imbalanced or complex data — exactly why it's your champion.
